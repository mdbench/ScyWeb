ScyWeb Core Concepts: The Decoherence Wall

A quantum attack on ScyWeb relies on maintaining complex, fault-tolerant superpositions over time. This visualization demonstrates how increasing environmental noise forces these fragile states to collapse, effectively capping the maximum useful depth \( T_d \) of a quantum calculation.

1. Fragile Superposition

A quantum system \( \psi \) begins in a clean superposition, free from noise. It can theoretically execute infinite fault-tolerant operations \( U \).

\[ |\psi(t=0)\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}}\sum_{i=0}^{N-1}|i\rangle \]

2. The Wall: Environment Interaction

As time \( t \) progresses, the environment \( E \) entangles with the system. This introduces noise \( \chi \), leading to information leakage and state fracture.

\[ |\psi(t)\rangle \otimes |E_0\rangle \xrightarrow{U} \sum c_i(t)|i\rangle \otimes |E_i(t)\rangle \]

3. The Wall: Decoherence (Collapse)

When noise exceeds the fault-tolerant threshold \( \chi > \sigma \), the system "decoheres." The off-diagonal terms vanish, and the system collapses into a classical state.

\[ \rho_{ij}(t) = \rho_{ij}(0) \cdot e^{-\chi(t)/\sigma} \xrightarrow{t > T_d} 0 \]
